## How to Use This Guide

Every recipe here uses **actual tea from your C&C pantry** — not extract, not flavoring. The tea is the star.

**Yields are standardized:**
- Single-batch = 12 portions (bite-size for tasting)
- Scale up × 2 for a 24-guest pop-up
- Scale up × 4 for a 50-guest flagship evening

**Three ways we infuse tea:**
1. **Steep into dairy** (cream, milk, butter) — best for custards, ganaches, ice creams
2. **Steep into syrup** (water + sugar) — best for glazes, poaching, soaks
3. **Grind into flour** (powdered matcha/hojicha) — best for doughs, financiers, cookies

### North Africa

#### 🌿 Mint-Glazed Semolina Cake (Meskouta bil-Na'na)
**Paired with:** Moroccan Mint
**Why:** Same mint, same sugar profile, same dusty Marrakech afternoon.

**Ingredients (12 portions)**

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------:|
| Fine semolina | 200 g |
| AP flour | 80 g |
| Caster sugar | 150 g |
| Baking powder | 10 g |
| Plain yogurt | 180 g |
| Neutral oil | 100 ml |
| Eggs | 2 |
| Orange zest | 1 orange |
| **For the mint glaze** | |
| Strong-brewed Moroccan Mint | 100 ml |
| Caster sugar | 80 g |
| Fresh spearmint leaves | 6–8 |

**Method**

1. Oven 180 °C / 356 °F. Grease a 22 cm round pan.
2. Whisk dry ingredients in one bowl, wet in another. Combine. Pour into pan.
3. Bake 28–32 min until a skewer comes out clean.
4. While baking, simmer the mint glaze ingredients 8 min until syrupy. Discard leaves.
5. Prick the hot cake with a skewer, pour glaze over. Let soak fully. Serve warm or room temp.

#### 🍯 Honey-Almond Briouats
**Paired with:** Saharan Tuareg
**Why:** Bold sugar echoes the triple-poured sugary mint of the Tuareg ceremony.

**Ingredients (makes 12)**

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------:|
| Warka / phyllo sheets | 6 |
| Ground almonds | 200 g |
| Powdered sugar | 60 g |
| Orange-blossom water | 1 tbsp |
| Ground cinnamon | ½ tsp |
| Butter (melted, for brushing) | 80 g |
| **Honey bath** | |
| Wildflower honey | 250 g |
| Orange-blossom water | 1 tsp |

**Method**

1. Mix almonds, powdered sugar, cinnamon, and orange-blossom water into a paste.
2. Cut phyllo into strips 8 cm wide. Brush with butter. Place a teaspoon of filling at one end.
3. Fold into triangles (like a flag). Seal edge with butter.
4. Fry in neutral oil at 170 °C / 338 °F until deep gold, ~2 min.
5. Warm honey with orange-blossom water. Dip fried briouats for 10 sec. Drain on rack.

#### 🌹 Rose & Pistachio Shortbread
**Paired with:** Moroccan Rosewater Tisane
**Why:** Rose echoes rose. Pistachio gives the green note that mint would otherwise provide.

**Ingredients (12 cookies)**

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------:|
| Butter (soft) | 180 g |
| Powdered sugar | 80 g |
| AP flour | 220 g |
| Ground pistachios | 40 g |
| Rosewater | 1 tsp |
| Dried rose petals (culinary) | 1 tbsp + extra to garnish |
| Salt | ¼ tsp |

**Method**

1. Cream butter + powdered sugar. Add rosewater, salt.
2. Fold in flour, pistachios, and crushed rose petals.
3. Chill 30 min. Roll into a log, slice 1 cm rounds.
4. Bake 160 °C / 320 °F for 14 min until pale gold.
5. Dust with powdered sugar, scatter rose petals.

### East Asia

#### 🍚 Genmaicha Panna Cotta with Toasted Rice Brittle
**Paired with:** Genmaicha
**Why:** The panna cotta extracts genmaicha's toasted-rice nuttiness; the brittle crystallizes it.

**Ingredients (12 small glasses)**

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------:|
| Heavy cream | 500 ml |
| Whole milk | 200 ml |
| Caster sugar | 80 g |
| Loose-leaf genmaicha | 12 g |
| Gelatin sheets | 4 (or 7 g powder) |
| Vanilla bean | ½ |
| **Brittle** | |
| Toasted rice (from genmaicha, reserved) | 30 g |
| Caster sugar | 100 g |
| Flaky salt | pinch |

**Method**

1. Warm cream + milk + sugar + vanilla to 70 °C. Off heat, steep genmaicha 8 min. Strain; reserve toasted rice bits.
2. Bloom gelatin in cold water, stir into warm cream to dissolve.
3. Pour into 60 ml glasses. Chill 4 h.
4. Brittle: dry-caramelize sugar until amber, stir in toasted rice + salt, pour onto parchment. Cool, crack into shards.
5. Top each panna cotta with a shard before serving.

#### 🍐 Oolong-Poached Pear with Honey-Miso Cream
**Paired with:** Alishan High Mountain Oolong
**Why:** Honeyed floral notes of Alishan marry a perfectly-poached pear.

**Ingredients (12 halves)**

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------:|
| Bosc pears (peeled, halved, cored) | 6 |
| Brewed Alishan oolong (strong) | 750 ml |
| Caster sugar | 200 g |
| Honey | 3 tbsp |
| Lemon zest | 1 strip |
| **Honey-miso cream** | |
| Mascarpone | 200 g |
| Heavy cream | 100 ml |
| White miso | 1 tsp |
| Honey | 2 tbsp |

**Method**

1. Bring oolong + sugar + honey + zest to gentle simmer. Poach pears 18–22 min until just tender.
2. Cool pears in liquid (soaks in flavor).
3. Whip mascarpone, cream, miso, and honey to soft peaks.
4. Serve pear half upright in a pool of reduced poaching syrup, crown with a dollop of cream.

#### ☕ Hojicha Tiramisu
**Paired with:** Hojicha
**Why:** Roasted, toasty hojicha replaces espresso. Darker, warmer, more contemplative tiramisu.

**Ingredients (12 glasses)**

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------:|
| Ladyfinger biscuits | 24 |
| Brewed hojicha (double-strength, cooled) | 250 ml |
| Mascarpone | 500 g |
| Heavy cream | 250 ml |
| Egg yolks | 4 |
| Caster sugar | 100 g |
| Hojicha powder (for dusting) | 3 tbsp |

**Method**

1. Whisk yolks + sugar over bain-marie until pale ribbon. Cool.
2. Fold in mascarpone. Whip cream to soft peaks, fold in.
3. Dip ladyfingers briefly in hojicha. Layer in glasses: biscuit → cream → biscuit → cream.
4. Chill overnight. Dust with hojicha powder before serving.

### South America

#### 🥛 Mate Alfajores (Dulce de Leche)
**Paired with:** Argentine Mate
**Why:** The quintessential Argentine pairing — mate in the gourd, alfajor on the plate.

**Ingredients (makes 12 sandwiches)**

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------:|
| Cornstarch | 240 g |
| AP flour | 160 g |
| Butter | 200 g |
| Powdered sugar | 150 g |
| Egg yolks | 3 |
| **Mate-infused dulce de leche** | |
| Dulce de leche (store-bought or homemade) | 500 g |
| Loose yerba mate | 8 g |
| Heavy cream | 2 tbsp |
| **Finish** | |
| Shredded coconut | 80 g |

**Method**

1. Sift cornstarch + flour. Cream butter + powdered sugar. Add yolks. Fold in flour mix.
2. Chill dough 1 h. Roll 5 mm thick, cut 5 cm rounds. Bake 170 °C / 338 °F for 12 min.
3. Mate DDL: warm cream, steep mate 6 min, strain. Whisk into DDL.
4. Sandwich cookies with DDL. Roll edges in coconut.

#### 🍫 Chimarrão Brigadeiros
**Paired with:** Chimarrão
**Why:** Brazilian truffles get a hit of vegetal chimarrão to cut the sweetness. Pure Rio Grande do Sul.

**Ingredients (makes 24 bites)**

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------:|
| Sweetened condensed milk | 400 g (1 can) |
| Butter | 30 g |
| Cocoa powder | 30 g |
| Finely ground chimarrão | 6 g |
| Dark chocolate (70%) | 80 g |
| **Coating** | |
| Cocoa nibs (crushed) | 60 g |

**Method**

1. Cook condensed milk + butter + cocoa + chimarrão over medium-low, stirring constantly, 12–15 min until it pulls from the pan.
2. Add chopped dark chocolate off heat. Stir to melt.
3. Pour onto buttered plate. Chill 1 h.
4. Roll into 15 g balls, coat in cacao nibs.

#### 🍮 Lemon Verbena & Chamomile Flan
**Paired with:** Mate Cocido de Hierbas
**Why:** Mirrors the soft herbal verbena & chamomile profile of the infusion.

**Ingredients (12 ramekins)**

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------:|
| Whole milk | 500 ml |
| Heavy cream | 250 ml |
| Dried lemon verbena | 6 g |
| Dried chamomile | 4 g |
| Caster sugar | 150 g |
| Whole eggs | 3 |
| Egg yolks | 3 |
| **Caramel** | |
| Caster sugar | 150 g |
| Water | 2 tbsp |

**Method**

1. Caramelize sugar to deep amber. Pour into ramekins, swirl to coat.
2. Warm milk + cream to 80 °C. Steep herbs 10 min. Strain.
3. Whisk eggs, yolks, and sugar. Temper in hot cream.
4. Pour into ramekins. Water bath 160 °C / 320 °F for 35–40 min.
5. Chill overnight. Invert to plate.

#### 🍋 Darjeeling Lemon Drizzle Cake
**Paired with:** Darjeeling First Flush
**Why:** The muscatel-citrus notes of first flush are amplified by real lemon.

**Ingredients (12 slices)**

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------:|

| Caster sugar | 220 g |
| Eggs | 4 |
| AP flour | 220 g |
| Baking powder | 8 g |
| Milk | 60 ml |
| Finely ground Darjeeling First Flush | 6 g |
| Lemon zest | 2 lemons |
| **Drizzle** | |
| Lemon juice | 80 ml |
| Caster sugar | 80 g |
| Brewed Darjeeling (strong, cooled) | 40 ml |

**Method**

1. Oven 170 °C / 338 °F. Line loaf tin.

#### 🌾 Saffron-Almond Kheer
**Paired with:** Kashmiri Kahwa
**Why:** Same saffron, same almond, same Kashmiri warmth — liquid-to-pudding transform.

**Ingredients (12 small bowls)**

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------:|
| Basmati rice (soaked) | 80 g |
| Whole milk | 1.5 L |
| Caster sugar | 120 g |
| Saffron threads | 20 threads |
| Green cardamom pods | 6 |
| Slivered almonds | 80 g |
| Pistachios (chopped) | 40 g |
| Rosewater | 1 tsp |

**Method**

1. Bloom saffron in 2 tbsp warm milk.
2. Simmer rice + milk + cardamom 40–50 min, stirring, until rice breaks and milk thickens.
3. Add sugar, almonds, saffron milk. Simmer 10 more min.
4. Finish with rosewater. Cool. Top with pistachios.

## 🔧 Universal Techniques

### 1. Infusing into Dairy

| Tea Type | Cream Temp | Steep Time | Notes |
|----------|-----------|:----------:|-------|
| Black (chai, Darjeeling) | 85 °C | 6–8 min | Strong enough to survive sugar |
| Green (genmaicha, sencha) | 70 °C | 4–5 min | Longer → bitter |
| Oolong (Alishan) | 80 °C | 6 min | Unlocks floral |
| Hojicha / roasted | 85 °C | 5–7 min | Very forgiving |
| Mate / chimarrão | 75 °C | 5 min | Never boil |
| Herbal (rooibos, hibiscus) | 90 °C | 8–10 min | Need heat & time |

### 2. Infusing into Syrup

Ratio: **1 g tea per 100 ml water + 100 g sugar**. Bring to simmer, steep off heat 8 min. Strain.

### 3. Tea Flour / Powder

- Grind loose leaf in a spice grinder 30 sec until fine.
- Sift through 60-mesh to remove large bits.
- Add **2–4 % of flour weight** for subtle flavor; **6–8 %** for assertive.

### 4. Tea Butter (Compound)

Soften 200 g butter, fold in 4 g finely ground tea + 1 tbsp honey. Roll in parchment, chill. Use for scones, financiers, brown-butter cookies.
