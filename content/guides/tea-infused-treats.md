## How to Use This Guide

Every recipe here uses **actual tea from your C&C pantry** — not extract, not flavoring. The tea is the star.

**Yields are standardized:**
- Single-batch = 12 portions (bite-size for tasting)
- Scale up × 2 for a 24-guest pop-up
- Scale up × 4 for a 50-guest flagship evening

**Three ways we infuse tea:**
1. **Steep into dairy** (cream, milk, butter) — best for custards, ganaches, ice creams
2. **Steep into syrup** (water + sugar) — best for glazes, poaching, soaks
3. **Grind into flour** (powdered matcha/hojicha) — best for doughs, financiers, cookies

## � Baking Fundamentals

Before any specific recipe, master these techniques. Most failures come from **bad pan prep** or **wrong mixing method** — not bad ingredients.

| **Half-sheet pans** | Universal workhorse. Buy 4. | Nordic Ware aluminum |
| **Parchment paper** | Pre-cut sheets save hours. | If You Care precut |

### 2. Pan Selection — Match the Pan to the Treat

| Recipe Type | Best Pan | Why |
|-------------|----------|-----|
| **Loaf cakes** (Darjeeling Lemon) | 9×5 in metal loaf pan, light-colored aluminum | Even heat, gentle browning. Glass over-bakes the bottom. |
| **Round cakes** (Meskouta) | 8 or 9 in round, 2-in tall, light aluminum | Even rise, easy release. Avoid dark non-stick — it browns crust before center cooks. |
| **Brownies** (Cacao-Mate) | 8×8 or 9×9 in **metal** square, bright finish | Bright metal = fudgy edges, gooey center. Ceramic = dry brick. |
| **Bar cookies / shortbread** (Chai Shortbread) | 8×8 or quarter-sheet, parchment-slung | Slings let you lift the slab whole, score before fully cool. |
| **Drop / log cookies** (Rose-Pistachio) | Half-sheet pan + Silpat | Air convection. Always rotate at 8 min. |
| **Phyllo / fried** (Briouats) | 12-in cast iron or heavy-bottomed Dutch oven | Holds 170 °C oil temp through batches. |
| **Custards / flans** (Verbena Flan, Saffron Kheer in dishes) | 6-oz ceramic ramekins **inside** a half-sheet (water bath) | Ceramic insulates, water bath = no curdling. |
| **Panna cotta / mousse** (Genmaicha PC) | 60–90 ml glass tumblers OR silicone pop molds | No baking — gelatin sets. Glass shows the layer. |
| **Trifles** (Silk Route) | 250 ml stemless wine glasses | Vertical layers visible — that's the show. |
| **Brittle / candy** (Genmaicha brittle) | Half-sheet + Silpat OR oiled marble | Cools fast and even. Never use parchment under hot caramel. |
| **Brigadeiros / truffles** | Plate or silicone mold | No bake. Mold for symmetry, plate for rustic. |
| **Sorbet** (Hibiscus-Rose) | Loaf pan or shallow stainless container, pre-frozen | Faster freezing in shallow pans. Plastic = ice crystals. |

**Aluminum vs. dark non-stick:** Aluminum reflects heat — recipes assume it. If you only have dark non-stick, **drop the temperature 25 °F** to compensate.

**Glass:** Heats slowly, retains heat. Drop temp 25 °F and check 5 min earlier.

### 3. Pan Prep — The Step Most People Skip

| Technique | When to Use | Method |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| **Grease only** | Drop cookies on Silpat | Skip. Silpat is enough. |
| **Grease + flour** | Round + bundt cakes (Meskouta) | Soft butter brushed everywhere → 1 tbsp flour, rotate to coat, tap out excess. |
| **Grease + cocoa** | Chocolate cakes & brownies | Same as flour, but with cocoa — no white residue on dark crumb. |
| **Parchment sling** | Brownies, bar cookies | Strip of parchment longer than pan, butter under it. Lifts the whole slab clean. |
| **Parchment round** | Round cakes (extra insurance) | Trace pan, cut, butter both sides. Combined with greased sides = guaranteed release. |
| **Caramel coat** | Flan / crème caramel | Pour molten caramel, swirl to coat bottom + ½ way up sides. Hardens before custard goes in. |
| **Butter + sugar** | Madeleines, financiers | Cold butter brushed, sprinkled with caster sugar. Crispy gold edge. |
| **Pre-chill** | Shortbread, laminated dough | Rolled dough → 20 min freezer before bake. Holds shape. |
| **Pre-freeze pan** | Sorbet | Container goes in freezer 1 h before scraping in. Shaves 30 min off setting time. |

**Always weigh** dough portions for cookies and bar slabs. Eye-balling = uneven bake.

### 4. The Six Mixing Methods

You only need to recognize these six. Every recipe in this guide uses one of them.

#### A. Creaming (cookies, butter cakes, shortbread)
**Used by:** Masala Chai Shortbread · Rose-Pistachio Shortbread · Mate Alfajores · Darjeeling Lemon Cake

1. **Butter at 18–20 °C** (cool but yields to finger pressure). Too cold = streaks. Too soft = greasy cookies.
2. Beat butter alone 30 sec until smooth.
3. Add sugar in two additions. Beat **3–4 min** at medium-high until pale and fluffy and visibly increased in volume. **This is non-negotiable** — you're whipping air into the fat. That trapped air is what makes the crumb tender.
4. Add eggs **one at a time**, fully incorporating each before the next. Add yolks last if separated.
5. Sift dry ingredients together so the leavener distributes.
6. Add dry on **low speed in 2–3 additions**, mixing only until streaks disappear. Stop the second you don't see flour. Over-mixing here = tough cookies.

#### B. Foaming / Ribbon Stage (sponges, panna cotta custard, tiramisu)
**Used by:** Hojicha Tiramisu · Silk Route Trifle (custard) · Verbena Flan

1. Eggs (or yolks) + sugar over a 60 °C bain-marie if heated, or at room temp if cold-method.
2. Whisk **5–7 minutes** at high speed until you can lift the whisk and the dripping ribbon holds its shape on the surface for 3–5 seconds before sinking. **This is "ribbon stage."** Volume should triple.
3. Sift any dry ingredients in 3 additions, folding gently with a spatula in figure-8 motion.
4. Bake **immediately** — air begins to deflate within 10 minutes.

#### C. Melt-and-Mix (brownies, blondies)
**Used by:** Cacao-Mate Brownie

1. Melt chocolate + butter together over a 50 °C bain-marie or in 30-second microwave bursts. Off heat the moment 80% is liquid — residual heat finishes it.
2. Whisk in both sugars vigorously. The hot fat dissolves crystals.
3. Cool 5 min before adding eggs (or they'll scramble).
4. Add eggs one at a time, whisking 30 sec each. **The shiny crackle top of a perfect brownie comes from the egg-and-sugar emulsion.** Don't skip this.
5. Fold in dry ingredients until just combined. Stop early.

#### D. Folding (mousses, sorbets, soufflés)
**Used by:** Hibiscus-Rose Sorbet · Hojicha Tiramisu (cream into mascarpone)

1. Always fold the **lighter** mixture **into the heavier**. Whipped cream into mascarpone, never the other way.
2. Add ⅓ of the lighter mixture first to "loosen" the heavy base — sacrifice it, mix in fully.
3. Add the rest in 2 additions. **Spatula technique:** cut down the center, sweep across the bottom, lift up the side, rotate the bowl ¼ turn. Repeat until streaks disappear.
4. Stop one rotation **before** you think it's done. Mixture continues to combine on its way to the pan.

#### E. Custard / Tempering (panna cotta, kheer, flans)
**Used by:** Genmaicha Panna Cotta · Saffron Kheer · Lemon Verbena Flan · Silk Route saffron custard

1. Heat the dairy with aromatics to **70–80 °C**, never above 85 °C.
2. Whisk yolks + sugar together separately until pale.
3. **Temper:** ladle ¼ cup of hot dairy into the yolks while whisking constantly. Add another ¼ cup. Now the yolk mixture is warm enough to return to the pan without scrambling.
4. Pour the tempered yolks back into the pot, stirring constantly with a spatula in figure-8s.
5. Cook over medium-low until **82–84 °C** on the probe thermometer or until it coats the back of a spoon and a finger drawn through the coating leaves a clean line. **Do not boil.**
6. Strain immediately through a fine sieve to catch any scrambled bits.

#### F. Roll & Cut (laminated dough, shortbread logs)
**Used by:** Mate Alfajores · Briouats · any cookie cut from sheet

1. Wrap dough disk-shaped (not ball) in plastic. Faster + even chilling.
2. Chill **at least 2 h, ideally overnight**. Gluten relaxes; butter firms.
3. Roll on a lightly floured surface. **Quarter-turn the dough every 2–3 passes** to prevent sticking.
4. Aim for even thickness — uneven dough = uneven bake. Use **rolling-pin guides** (silicone bands) if struggling.
5. After cutting, transfer cuts to a parchment-lined sheet and **freeze 15 minutes before baking** — locks the edges so cookies hold their shape.

### 5. Setting in the Pan

#### Cake Batters
- Pour batter to **fill ⅔ of pan height**. More than that and it overflows; less and it bakes too thin.
- Tap the filled pan firmly on the counter 3 times to release air pockets.
- For an even-domed cake, smooth the top and run the spatula from edge to edge so the **center is slightly lower than the edges** — heat hits sides first and pushes center up to flat.

#### Bar / Slab Doughs (shortbread, brownie)
- Use **damp fingertips** to press evenly. Dry fingers stick.
- For shortbread: press to corners, then **score the cuts before baking** — clean lines once cool.
- For brownies: smooth, then tap pan twice to settle.

#### Cookie Doughs
- **Weigh portions** (15 g for tasting, 30 g for full cookie).
- Space **5 cm / 2 in apart** on the sheet. They spread.
- For tall cookies that hold shape: chill the portioned balls 30 min before baking.

#### Custards
- Strain into a pitcher first, then pour into ramekins. Catches stray strands of egg + bubbles.
- Place ramekins in a deep half-sheet. Pour **boiling water into the sheet** until it reaches **⅔ up the ramekin sides**. (Pour the water in **after** the pan is on the oven rack to avoid splashing into the custard.)
- Cover loosely with foil if you see surfaces browning before set.

#### Phyllo / Fried
- Preheat oil to **170 °C / 338 °F**. Use a thermometer — eye-balling fails.
- Fry no more than 4 pieces at once (drops the oil temp).
- Drain on a wire rack over paper towel. **Never directly on paper** — soggy bottoms.

### 6. Baking Temperature & Time Rules

| Treat Type | Standard Temp | Rule of Thumb |
|------------|---------------|---------------|
| **Shortbread / butter cookies** | 160–170 °C / 320–340 °F | Low & slow → pale gold, no browning |
| **Drop cookies** | 175–180 °C / 350–360 °F | Standard. Rotate at 8 min. |
| **Loaf cakes** | 165–170 °C / 330–340 °F | Lower than a layer cake — slower bake to center |
| **Layer / round cakes** | 175 °C / 350 °F | Standard |
| **Brownies (fudgy)** | 170 °C / 338 °F | Pull when center jiggles slightly |
| **Brownies (cakey)** | 180 °C / 360 °F | Add 5 min |
| **Panna cotta** | No bake | Refrigerate ≥ 4 h |
| **Custard / flan (water bath)** | 150–160 °C / 300–320 °F | Slow & gentle. Done when edges set, center jiggles like Jell-O. |
| **Phyllo / briouats (oil)** | 170 °C / 338 °F | Steady oil temp = even crisp |
| **Sponge / ladyfingers** | 175 °C / 350 °F | Fast bake to lock the air |

**Always preheat 20 minutes minimum.** Convection ovens lie about being ready. Use an oven thermometer.

**Rack position:**
- Center rack for everything single-pan.
- Two pans = upper-third + lower-third, swap halfway.
- Custards = always center-rack water bath.

### 7. Doneness Cues — Trust These, Not the Timer

| Treat | Visual Cue | Touch Cue | Internal |
|-------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Shortbread** | Pale gold edges, center matte | Firm to gentle press | — |
| **Drop cookies** | Edges set, center looks **slightly under** | Springs back at edge, soft center | — |
| **Loaf cake** | Top dome cracked, golden | Skewer through center → moist crumbs OK, no wet batter | 96 °C |
| **Brownie (fudgy)** | Top crackled and shiny, edges pull from pan | Center jiggles slightly when pan shaken | 88–90 °C |
| **Brownie (cakey)** | Top dry, no jiggle | Skewer comes out with a few crumbs | 96 °C |
| **Custard / flan** | Edges set, center wobbles like Jell-O when shaken | Don't poke — disturbs surface | 76–78 °C |
| **Panna cotta** | (Set in fridge) — wobbles when tapped, holds shape | — | — |

**If the timer ends and your cue says no:** trust the cue. Set a 2-minute timer and re-check.

### 8. Cooling — As Important as Baking

| Treat | Cool Time in Pan | Then |
|-------|------------------|------|
| Cakes | 10 min | Invert onto wire rack, peel parchment, cool fully (1 h) before glazing |
| Brownies | 30 min in pan | Lift parchment sling onto board, refrigerate 2 h before clean cuts |
| Shortbread | 5 min | Re-score the cuts (warm), then transfer to rack |
| Cookies | 3 min | Transfer to rack — letting them sit longer = soggy bottoms |
| Custard / flan | Cool to room temp 1 h | Then refrigerate **overnight** for clean unmolding |
| Panna cotta | Set 4 h minimum | Quick dip mold in warm water 5 sec to release |

**Never cut warm.** Brownies, bars, cakes — patience here is the difference between professional and amateur.

### 9. Common Failures & Fixes

| Problem | Cause | Fix Next Time |
|---------|-------|---------------|
| **Sunken cake center** | Under-baked or too much leavener | Bake 5 more min OR reduce baking powder by ¼ tsp |
| **Tough cake / cookies** | Over-mixed flour (gluten dev) | Add dry on low, stop at "just combined" |
| **Greasy cookies** | Butter too soft or oven too low | Butter at 18 °C (cool but yields). Use oven thermometer. |
| **Cracked custard surface** | Oven too hot | Drop 10 °C, check water bath water level |
| **Curdled custard** | Custard exceeded 85 °C | Strain immediately. Whisk vigorously. Use lower heat. |
| **Brownie dry/cakey when wanted fudgy** | Over-baked | Pull when center jiggles. Internal 88 °C max. |
| **Cookie spread too thin** | Butter too warm or pan too hot | Chill cut cookies 15 min before baking. Cool pan between batches. |
| **Phyllo soggy** | Oil under 165 °C | Use thermometer. Bring back to 170 °C between batches. |
| **Sorbet icy** | Not enough sugar or churned too long | Stick to ratios. Pull when soft-serve consistency, freeze to firm. |
| **Tea flavor weak** | Not enough tea / not steeped long enough | Double the tea quantity OR steep covered 8 min minimum |
| **Tea flavor bitter** | Steeped too long or temp too high | Refer to dairy steep table — green tea 70 °C, 4 min max |

### North Africa

#### 🌿 Mint-Glazed Semolina Cake (Meskouta bil-Na'na)
**Paired with:** Moroccan Mint
**Why:** Same mint, same sugar profile, same dusty Marrakech afternoon.

**Ingredients (12 portions)**

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------:|
| Fine semolina | 200 g |
| AP flour | 80 g |
| Caster sugar | 150 g |
| Baking powder | 10 g |
| Plain yogurt | 180 g |
| Neutral oil | 100 ml |
| Eggs | 2 |
| Orange zest | 1 orange |
| **For the mint glaze** | |
| Strong-brewed Moroccan Mint | 100 ml |
| Caster sugar | 80 g |
| Fresh spearmint leaves | 6–8 |

**Method**

1. Oven 180 °C / 356 °F. Grease a 22 cm round pan.
2. Whisk dry ingredients in one bowl, wet in another. Combine. Pour into pan.
3. Bake 28–32 min until a skewer comes out clean.
4. While baking, simmer the mint glaze ingredients 8 min until syrupy. Discard leaves.
5. Prick the hot cake with a skewer, pour glaze over. Let soak fully. Serve warm or room temp.

#### 🍯 Honey-Almond Briouats
**Paired with:** Saharan Tuareg
**Why:** Bold sugar echoes the triple-poured sugary mint of the Tuareg ceremony.

**Ingredients (makes 12)**

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------:|
| Warka / phyllo sheets | 6 |
| Ground almonds | 200 g |
| Powdered sugar | 60 g |
| Orange-blossom water | 1 tbsp |
| Ground cinnamon | ½ tsp |
| Butter (melted, for brushing) | 80 g |
| **Honey bath** | |
| Wildflower honey | 250 g |
| Orange-blossom water | 1 tsp |

**Method**

1. Mix almonds, powdered sugar, cinnamon, and orange-blossom water into a paste.
2. Cut phyllo into strips 8 cm wide. Brush with butter. Place a teaspoon of filling at one end.
3. Fold into triangles (like a flag). Seal edge with butter.
4. Fry in neutral oil at 170 °C / 338 °F until deep gold, ~2 min.
5. Warm honey with orange-blossom water. Dip fried briouats for 10 sec. Drain on rack.

### East Asia

#### 🍚 Genmaicha Panna Cotta with Toasted Rice Brittle
**Paired with:** Genmaicha
**Why:** The panna cotta extracts genmaicha's toasted-rice nuttiness; the brittle crystallizes it.

**Ingredients (12 small glasses)**

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------:|
| Heavy cream | 500 ml |
| Whole milk | 200 ml |
| Caster sugar | 80 g |
| Loose-leaf genmaicha | 12 g |
| Gelatin sheets | 4 (or 7 g powder) |
| Vanilla bean | ½ |
| **Brittle** | |
| Toasted rice (from genmaicha, reserved) | 30 g |
| Caster sugar | 100 g |
| Flaky salt | pinch |

**Method**

1. Warm cream + milk + sugar + vanilla to 70 °C. Off heat, steep genmaicha 8 min. Strain; reserve toasted rice bits.
2. Bloom gelatin in cold water, stir into warm cream to dissolve.
3. Pour into 60 ml glasses. Chill 4 h.
4. Brittle: dry-caramelize sugar until amber, stir in toasted rice + salt, pour onto parchment. Cool, crack into shards.
5. Top each panna cotta with a shard before serving.

## 🔧 Universal Techniques

### 1. Infusing into Dairy

| Tea Type | Cream Temp | Steep Time | Notes |
|----------|-----------|:----------:|-------|
| Black (chai, Darjeeling) | 85 °C | 6–8 min | Strong enough to survive sugar |
| Green (genmaicha, sencha) | 70 °C | 4–5 min | Longer → bitter |
| Oolong (Alishan) | 80 °C | 6 min | Unlocks floral |
| Hojicha / roasted | 85 °C | 5–7 min | Very forgiving |
| Mate / chimarrão | 75 °C | 5 min | Never boil |
| Herbal (rooibos, hibiscus) | 90 °C | 8–10 min | Need heat & time |

### 2. Infusing into Syrup

Ratio: **1 g tea per 100 ml water + 100 g sugar**. Bring to simmer, steep off heat 8 min. Strain.

### 3. Tea Flour / Powder

- Grind loose leaf in a spice grinder 30 sec until fine.
- Sift through 60-mesh to remove large bits.
- Add **2–4 % of flour weight** for subtle flavor; **6–8 %** for assertive.

### 4. Tea Butter (Compound)

Soften 200 g butter, fold in 4 g finely ground tea + 1 tbsp honey. Roll in parchment, chill. Use for scones, financiers, brown-butter cookies.
