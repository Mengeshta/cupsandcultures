## How to Use This Guide

Each fusion recipe is written in **parts by weight** so you can scale from a single cup to a 50-guest event. Measurements are in **grams** for accuracy, with teaspoon equivalents for convenience.

**Standard ratios:**
- 1 cup (8 oz / 240 ml) = ~2.5 g tea blend
- 4-cup pot (32 oz) = ~10 g tea blend
- 12-person event batch = ~60–75 g tea blend

### 1. Serengeti Gold

> *Southern Africa × Maghreb — Where the red sands of the Kalahari meet the high Atlas.*

**Profile:** Earthy · Sweet · Mint · Light body
**Caffeine:** None
**Wellness:** Antioxidants · digestive · calming

#### Recipe (makes 1 tin · ~75 g · ~30 cups)

| Ingredient | Grams | Parts | tsp (approx.) |
|------------|------:|------:|--------------:|
| Organic rooibos | 45 g | 6 | ~18 tsp |
| Dried spearmint | 15 g | 2 | ~10 tsp |
| Dried rose petals | 8 g | 1 | ~4 tsp |
| Cinnamon chips (small) | 4 g | ½ | ~2 tsp |
| Orange-blossom honey (drizzled in-cup) | to taste | — | — |

#### Brew

- **Temp:** 95 °C / 203 °F
- **Steep:** 5–7 minutes (rooibos likes long)
- **Ratio:** 2.5 g per 8 oz cup
- **Finish:** ½ tsp orange-blossom honey stirred in at service

### 2. Silk Route

> *Himalayas × Kashmir × Persia — A caravan in a cup.*

**Profile:** Floral · Spiced · Light-medium body
**Caffeine:** Yes (Darjeeling base)
**Wellness:** Circulation · anti-inflammatory (saffron) · mood-lifting

#### Recipe (makes 1 tin · ~75 g · ~30 cups)

| Ingredient | Grams | Parts | tsp (approx.) |
|------------|------:|------:|--------------:|
| Darjeeling First Flush | 50 g | 10 | ~25 tsp |
| Green cardamom pods (lightly crushed) | 10 g | 2 | ~4 tsp |
| Dried rose petals | 8 g | 1½ | ~4 tsp |
| Cinnamon chips | 4 g | ½ | ~2 tsp |
| **Saffron threads** (pinch per pot, not in blend) | — | — | 4–6 threads per 8 oz |

> ⚠️ **Don't blend saffron into the tin** — it loses potency. Add threads directly to hot water at brewing.

#### Brew

- **Temp:** 85 °C / 185 °F
- **Steep:** 4 minutes
- **Ratio:** 2.5 g blend + 4–6 saffron threads per 8 oz
- **Finish:** Float one rose petal on surface for presentation

### 3. Obsidian Mate

> *Pampas × Kyoto — The gaucho's mate meets the Kyoto hearth.*

**Profile:** Earthy · Roasted · Full body · Bitter-forward
**Caffeine:** Yes (mate + hojicha mild)
**Wellness:** Sustained energy · polyphenols · focus

#### Recipe (makes 1 tin · ~80 g · ~32 cups)

| Ingredient | Grams | Parts | tsp (approx.) |
|------------|------:|------:|--------------:|
| Argentine yerba mate | 40 g | 5 | ~12 tsp |
| Hojicha (roasted green) | 24 g | 3 | ~10 tsp |
| Cacao nibs (raw) | 12 g | 1½ | ~5 tsp |
| Cinnamon chips | 4 g | ½ | ~2 tsp |

#### Brew

- **Temp:** 80 °C / 176 °F *(any hotter scorches mate and turns it bitter)*
- **Steep:** 3–4 minutes
- **Ratio:** 2.5 g per 8 oz
- **Gongfu option:** 5 g in a 150 ml gaiwan, 30 sec infusions, 4–5 pours

## 💡 Storage, Shelf Life & Pro Tips

### Storage
- **Airtight glass jars, kept dark and cool** (pantry shelf, not fridge).
- **Never store with strong-smelling foods** — tea absorbs odors aggressively.
- **Label each tin** with: fusion name, blend date, batch number.

### Shelf Life
| Blend Type | Fresh Window |
|-----------|:-----:|
| Rooibos, hibiscus, herbal fusions | 12–18 months |
| Black tea fusions (Silk Route, Serengeti if blended w/ base) | 12 months |
| Oolong & green fusions (Monsoon Bloom, Obsidian Mate) | 6–9 months |
| Saffron | Keep separate — 2 years sealed, 6 months opened |

### Pro Tips

1. **Blend small batches weekly** rather than large monthly batches — freshness shows in the cup.
2. **Always weigh, never eyeball** — the radar-chart flavor profile only holds if ratios are consistent.
3. **Taste-test each batch against a control** — keep a 10 g "reference" from your best batch and compare.
4. **Pre-portion event tins** into single-serve 2.5 g sachets for pop-ups — faster service, less waste.
5. **Saffron and cacao nibs go in-cup, not in-tin** — they lose aroma in blends over time.
6. **Photograph every new batch** for your Instagram @cupsandcultures.co — the ritual *is* the brand.
7. **Keep a brewing log** — note water hardness, temperature, steep time, and guest reactions.
